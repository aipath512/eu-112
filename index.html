The 5 Operational
Blocks

Direct answer: The first B2B priority is machine-readable transparency: AI disclosure, traceability, responsibility, governance and evidence.

For most B2B firms, the first EU AI Act operational priority is machine-readable transparency and evidence.

01

AI Disclosure

Clearly indicate AI-generated content, AI-assisted support, AI recommendations, AI summaries, voice, video and image generation.

02

Traceability

Show timestamps, versions, provenance, origin, approvals, and the model or system that generated the output.

03

Responsibility

Define legal entity, representative, governance owner, contact point and escalation path.

04

Machine-readable Governance

Create structured AI policies, machine-readable declarations, provenance endpoints, transparency metadata and semantic trust layers.

05

Evidence

Move from “we claim” to “we can demonstrate” through records, proofs, logs, approvals and verifiable documentation.

What is EU-112™?

EU-112™ is an operational guide for EU AI Act readiness. DIY first, personalized help optional.

Is EU-112™ legal advice?

No. EU-112™ provides operational implementation guidance, not legal advice, certification, or notified body services.

Who is EU-112™ for?

EU-112™ is for firm owners, freelancers, SMBs, agencies and operational teams across Europe.

What is the first operational step?

The first operational step is to identify where AI is used, who is responsible, and what must be disclosed.

What does machine-readable transparency mean?

Machine-readable transparency means AI governance, evidence and declarations can be understood by both humans and machines.

What is AI inventory?

An AI inventory lists AI tools, providers, users, inputs, outputs, risks and responsibilities.

What is AI governance?

AI governance defines responsibility, oversight, escalation paths and operational controls.

What is AI evidence?

AI evidence includes logs, versions, timestamps, provenance, approvals and traceability records.

Can firms start alone?

Yes. EU-112™ is DIY-first. Personalized help is optional when implementation becomes complex.

What is the business benefit?

Firms become easier to inspect, trust, document and prepare for procurement or regulatory questions.
